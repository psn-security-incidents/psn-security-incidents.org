Are you REALLY communicating with Sony Support?

A hacker can spoof the 1-800 tech support number to call you

They will pose as PlayStation support offering to help recover your account. This is a social engineering attack designed to extract more information or distract and delay any actions you might otherwise take to recover your account.

BBB Cases & Callback Verification

Raising a case with the BBB may either generate a call to you on the phone number you left at the BBB, or generate an email to the email address you filled out at the BBB saying a call has been attempted.

  • › Inspect the sender address carefully. Modern email providers like Gmail will generally block spoofed sony.com addresses, but attackers use domains that look close — for example sony-support.com, playstation-account.com, or sonysupport.net. Check the full "From" address, not just the display name. If in doubt, paste the full email headers into ChatGPT and ask if they are legitimate.
  • › Watch for overlap. If the phone number or email you left with the BBB is also on your Sony account, it is possible that the call is not from Sony — the hacker may have access to your account contact details.
  • › Challenge the caller. Ask them to quote the case number from the Sony.com BBB-related email. If they cannot quote that case number, hang up immediately.
  • › Call Sony back yourself. Call Sony on their official support number and ask to be put through to the agent who called you, referencing the case number. This confirms you are speaking to a real agent.

Never communicate with Sony support outside of sony.com email. Only answer questions about your PSN account if you are 100% sure you are talking to a Sony account restoration agent.

Sounding Legitimate Is Not Enough

Just because an agent sounds legitimate does not mean they are legit. Hackers will employ un-accented people to make convincing and authoritative support calls.

If they cannot use a call or email to get information and widen the attack, they will distract and delay with false promises while the actual Sony and BBB cases are being closed — or are closed by the hacker themselves via impersonation of you and your account.
